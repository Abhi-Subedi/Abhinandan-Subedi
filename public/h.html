Morning routine — 15 minutes total
🚿 Shower — cold or lukewarm 7 min
1. Wet entire body. Use a body wash or soap — work from top to bottom.
2. Focus on armpits, groin, feet, behind ears — these are the odor zones most people miss.
3. Shampoo hair only if washing that day (not every day — see weekly tab).
4. End with 30 seconds of cold water. Closes pores, wakes your brain, reduces body odor.
Cold finish = less odor throughout day
🫧 Face wash 1 min
1. Use a gentle face wash — not soap, not body wash. Face skin is different.
2. Wet face, apply face wash, massage gently in circles for 30 seconds.
3. Rinse with cold water. Pat dry — never rub. Rubbing causes irritation.
→ Never pop pimples with dirty hands. It spreads bacteria and causes scarring.
→ Change your pillowcase every 3–4 days. Your face rests on it for 7 hours.
→ Don't over-wash. Washing more than twice a day strips natural oils and makes acne worse.
→ Drink more water. Seriously. Most teenage acne is made worse by dehydration.
Touch your face less during the day
💧 Moisturizer (optional but smart) 30 sec
Apply a small amount of light moisturizer after washing face. Even if skin feels normal now — moisturizer prevents early aging and keeps skin balanced. Use one that says "non-comedogenic" (won't clog pores).

Takes 30 seconds, makes a visible difference in 2 weeks
🦷 Teeth — the most underrated hygiene habit 3 min
1. Brush for a full 2 minutes — most people do 30 seconds. Use a timer once to check yourself.
2. Brush tongue. Bad breath is 80% tongue bacteria, not teeth.
3. Rinse with mouthwash for 30 seconds if you have it.
Your smile and breath are the first things people notice when you speak. You want to improve communication skills — bad breath destroys confidence in conversation before you say a single word. This is non-negotiable.
🧴 Deodorant — immediately after shower 30 sec
Apply to clean, dry armpits right after showering — before you get dressed. This is the correct order. Applying over sweat or damp skin doesn't work properly.

Apply before getting dressed, not after Reapply midday if needed at college
✂️ Nails + appearance check 1 min
Quick check in the mirror before leaving. Clean nails, no visible dirt, collar straight, hair set. People judge appearance in 3 seconds. You're building a public presence — this matters more than people admit.
Night routine — 10 minutes total
🦷 Brush teeth again — more important than morning 3 min
Night brushing is actually more important than morning. During the day you eat, drink, accumulate bacteria. If you sleep without brushing, bacteria sit on your teeth for 8 hours. That's what causes cavities and gum disease.

1. Brush 2 minutes + tongue.
2. Floss at least 3x a week — gum disease starts between teeth where brushes don't reach.
3. Mouthwash optional but good.
🫧 Face wash — remove the day 1 min
Same as morning. Removes sweat, pollution, and oil that built up during the day. If you skip this, all of that sits on your skin while you sleep — directly into your pillow, then back onto your face. This is why acne gets worse.
💧 Moisturizer at night 30 sec
Night is when skin repairs itself. A light moisturizer before sleep works with your body's natural repair cycle. You'll notice clearer, smoother skin within 2–3 weeks of doing this consistently.
📵 Phone away 30 min before sleep Important
This is hygiene too — sleep hygiene. Blue light from screens tells your brain it's daytime, suppresses melatonin, and delays sleep by up to 2 hours. Poor sleep causes more acne, more body odor, worse skin, and slower thinking the next day. Everything connects.

Bad sleep = worse skin + worse everything
Weekly schedule
💇Hair wash
2–3x per week — not daily. Daily washing strips natural oils, makes scalp produce more oil, and causes dandruff over time. Wash Monday, Wednesday, Saturday for example.
✂️Nail trim
Once a week — both hands and feet. Dirty nails carry more bacteria than a toilet seat. People notice this, especially when you're talking or gesturing.
👅Tongue scraper
Daily or weekly — if you don't have one, use your toothbrush on your tongue. Removes the white coating that causes 80% of bad breath.
👕Clean clothes
Underwear daily — non-negotiable. Socks daily. Outer clothes every 2 days minimum. Wearing clean-smelling clothes after a shower and then putting on yesterday's shirt cancels everything.
🛏️Pillowcase change
Every 3–4 days — your face presses on this for 7 hours. Sweat, oil, bacteria transfer directly to skin. This single habit reduces acne significantly.
🏃Exercise
3–5x per week — sweating flushes pores. Exercise improves skin, reduces body odor long-term by improving metabolism, and regulates hormones that cause acne.
💧Water intake — daily
8 glasses (2 litres) minimum per day. Dehydration is the hidden cause behind most teenage acne, body odor, and fatigue. If your urine is dark yellow — drink more. It should be pale yellow or clear.

Most skin problems improve with just water
